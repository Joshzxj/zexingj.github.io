How to read paper

Posted on Oct 20, 2023

#research #methodology

Three Pass Reading

The first pass gives you a general idea about the paper. The second pass lets you grasp the paper’s content, but not its details. The third pass helps you understand the paper in depth.

The First Pass:

  • Title, abstract and introduction
  • Section and sub-section headings. Ignore everything else
  • Read the conclusions
  • Glance over the references, mentally ticking off the one that already read

At the end of the first pass, should be able to answer:

  • Category: what the type of the paper is. measurement paper Analysis existing system? Description of a research prototype?
  • Context: what’s the relationship with other paper? which theroectial based were used to analyze the problem
  • Correctness: asusmption vaild?
  • Contributions: what’s the main contributions
  • Clarity

The Second Pass

  • Figures, diagrams and illustration in the paper
  • Remember to make unread reference for further reading

The Third Pass

  • Making the same assumption as the authors, recreate the work

You should identify and challenge every assumption in every statement. Moreover, you should think about how you yourself would present a particular idea. This comparison of the actual with the virtual lends a sharp insight into the proof and presentation techniques in the paper and you can very likely add this to your repertoire of tools. During this pass, you should also jot down ideas for future work.

At the end of this pass, you should be able to reconstruct the entire structure of the paper from memory, as well as be able to identify its strong and weak points.

My Workflow for Paper Reading

  • Import electronic version into reader
  • Summarize document
    • What is the problem to solve
    • What is the impact in this area
    • How to evaluate the paper
  • Decide whether need to moving forward
  • Print this paper
  • Understand More background
    • How this paper has been illustrated
    • Write down question
    • Solve those question by reading paper
  • Decide whether need to moving forward
    • Implementation this paper in your brain
    • From reviewer perspective
    • Fig out everything
